What Cookies Are

Cookies are small files stored on a device when someone visits a website. Similar technologies include pixels, local storage, tags, and SDKs.

Cookie Categories

  • Necessary cookies: required for security, forms, login, cart, checkout, and basic site operation.
  • Functional cookies: remember preferences such as saved settings or dismissed notices.
  • Analytics cookies: help measure site traffic and improve content.
  • Marketing cookies: may support advertising, retargeting, or campaign measurement if those tools are added.

Cookie Consent

Visitors can accept, reject, or manage non-essential cookies using the cookie banner. The prototype stores the visitor’s cookie choice in local browser storage. A production site should connect consent choices to the actual analytics and advertising scripts being used.

Examples of Tools That May Use Cookies

  • Google Analytics or similar analytics tools
  • Meta Pixel or similar advertising tools
  • Email signup forms and newsletter platforms
  • Login/session tools
  • Shopping cart and checkout tools
  • Embedded video or audio players

Managing Cookies

Visitors may also manage cookies through their browser settings. Blocking necessary cookies may affect website functionality.
